Leave No Trace Principles

Leave No Trace is a set of outdoor ethics designed to protect wild spaces for future visitors and the wildlife that calls them home. The core idea is simple: your visit should leave the landscape looking exactly as you found it, or better. When every hiker makes small, thoughtful choices, the combined impact is enormous.

In practice, this means packing out everything you brought in — including food wrappers, fruit peels, and any other waste — and leaving natural objects like rocks, wildflowers, and pinecones exactly where you find them. Each element plays a role in the local ecosystem, and even small removals add up when multiplied across thousands of visitors.

Trail Impact Reduction

Staying on marked and designated trails is one of the most effective ways to protect the land around you. Wandering off-path — even just a few feet — tramples fragile vegetation, compacts soil, and creates erosion that widens over time. Switchbacks are designed to reduce slope damage, so cutting them causes real harm even when it looks like a harmless shortcut.

Keeping noise levels low is equally important, both for wildlife and for fellow hikers seeking a peaceful experience. Loud music or shouting can disrupt animals during feeding, nesting, or resting — and it takes away the natural quiet that makes the outdoors worth visiting in the first place.

Wildlife Responsibility

Always maintain a safe, respectful distance from any animal you encounter on the trail. A simple rule: if an animal changes its behavior because of your presence — moving away, freezing, or staring — you are already too close. This applies to all wildlife, from small birds and deer to bears and mountain lions.

Never feed wildlife, even animals that seem small or friendly. Human food is harmful to animals' health, and feeding them conditions them to associate people with food — a behavior that frequently leads to dangerous encounters and the removal or euthanasia of the animal. Observe quietly from a distance, and always let the animal's comfort take priority over your curiosity.
